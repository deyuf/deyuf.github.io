About

Passionate about

I work at the intersection of systems and AI — pushing the boundaries of what's possible when hardware meets cutting-edge machine learning.

I'm a Senior Developer Technology Engineer on NVIDIA's Deep Learning Frameworks team, where I focus on accelerating deep learning training workloads across the full software stack — from CUDA kernels and math libraries to communication backends, ML frameworks, and production-scale models.

My recent work centers on NVIDIA Merlin Distributed Embeddings and large-scale recommender systems, enabling training of models with 100 B+ parameters efficiently on multi-GPU clusters.

I completed my graduate studies at the Technical University of Munich (TUM), where I also conducted research in computer vision, focusing on 3D object detection for autonomous driving using monocular cameras and sim-to-real domain adaptation techniques.

Research

Selected publications

Work at the boundary of AI systems and applied machine learning.

IEEE ITSC 2024 · Conference Paper

WARM-3D: A Weakly-Supervised Sim2Real Domain Adaptation Framework for Roadside Monocular 3D Object Detection

Deyu Fu et al. · IEEE 27th International Conference on Intelligent Transportation Systems, 2024

A weakly-supervised framework for bridging the synthetic-to-real domain gap in monocular 3D object detection deployed on roadside infrastructure. The method enables accurate depth estimation and object localization without requiring expensive LiDAR annotations, leveraging simulation data and weak supervision signals for robust real-world deployment.

NVIDIA Technical Blog · 2022

Fast, Terabyte-Scale Recommender Training Made Easy with NVIDIA Merlin Distributed-Embeddings

Deyu Fu et al. · NVIDIA Developer Blog, August 2022

Presents NVIDIA Merlin Distributed Embeddings — a library enabling scalable training of recommender system models with over 100 billion parameters across multi-GPU and multi-node clusters. The work demonstrates significant throughput improvements for embedding-heavy workloads using custom CUDA kernels and optimized collective communication patterns.
